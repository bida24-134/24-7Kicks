LEGENDARY SEASON

The Impossible Treble: Manchester United's 1999 Triumph

The 1998-99 Manchester United home kit, manufactured by Umbro with the iconic Sharp sponsorship, witnessed one of football's greatest achievements. Sir Alex Ferguson's men completed an unprecedented treble, winning the Premier League, FA Cup, and UEFA Champions League in a single season.

The Premier League race went down to the final day, with United edging Arsenal by a single point. In the FA Cup final, they defeated Newcastle United 2-0. But it was the Champions League final that cemented this team in football folklore.

Trailing 1-0 to Bayern Munich in the 90th minute, United scored twice in injury time through substitutes Teddy Sheringham and Ole Gunnar Solskjaer, creating what many consider the most dramatic comeback in football history. This simple red Umbro design became immortalized as the kit worn during football's most improbable turnaround.

France's First World Cup Glory

The French national team's 1998 home kit by Nike became synonymous with their first-ever World Cup victory on home soil. Led by captain Didier Deschamps and the brilliant Zinedine Zidane, Les Bleus dominated the tournament, culminating in a 3-0 final victory over Brazil. Zidane's two headers in the final while wearing this iconic blue jersey created one of the World Cup's most memorable moments.

TRANSITION ERA

Liverpool's Spice Boys Era

Liverpool's 1996-98 home kit with its distinctive white pinstripes became associated with the "Spice Boys" era. Despite featuring talents like Robbie Fowler, Steve McManaman, and Michael Owen, this team became known as much for their off-field personas as their playing style. While they reached the 1996 FA Cup final in this kit, their notorious pre-match white suits overshadowed their performance.

1988-1989
EUROPEAN DOMINANCE

Sacchi's Milan Revolution

AC Milan's 1988-89 home kit marked the beginning of a golden era under Arrigo Sacchi. The legendary Dutch trio of Ruud Gullit, Marco van Basten, and Frank Rijkaard dominated European football in this iconic red and black striped jersey. The team demolished Steaua Bucharest 4-0 in the European Cup final, with van Basten and Gullit both scoring twice while wearing this Kappa-manufactured classic.

1995-1996
INFAMOUS MOMENT

The Kit That "Couldn't Be Seen"

Manchester United's grey away kit from 1995-96 became infamous when Sir Alex Ferguson ordered his team to change outfits at half-time against Southampton, claiming players couldn't see each other. United were losing 3-0 at the break, switched to their blue and white third kit, but still lost 3-1. The grey kit was never worn again, creating one of football's most memorable kit controversies.

This was the first Brazil World Cup kit manufactured by Adidas, featuring their iconic three stripes on the sleeves.
